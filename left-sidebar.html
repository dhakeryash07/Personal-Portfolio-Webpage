Real-Time Positioning in Satellite Navigation

Real-time positioning in satellite navigation involves continuously determining the location of a receiver using signals from satellites. By analyzing the time delay between the satellite signal transmission and its reception, the receiver calculates the distance to multiple satellites. This data is then used to pinpoint the receiver’s position on Earth. In my ISRO internship, I focused on improving the accuracy of real-time positioning for GNSS systems by implementing a Kalman filter framework, which reduced errors and enhanced the precision of satellite-based navigation systems.
